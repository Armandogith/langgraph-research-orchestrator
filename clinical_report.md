# Clinical Evidence Report: Semaglutide for Obesity in Patients with Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)

## Executive Summary

**Background:** Chronic kidney disease (CKD) affects approximately 10% of the global population, and obesity is a major modifiable risk factor for CKD progression and cardiovascular complications. Semaglutide, a glucagon-like peptide-1 receptor agonist (GLP-1 RA), has emerged as a promising therapeutic option for weight management and metabolic improvement. This report evaluates the latest evidence (2022–2024) on the efficacy and safety of semaglutide for obesity management in patients with CKD.

**Key Findings:**
- Semaglutide demonstrates significant nephroprotective effects, including a **24% reduction in the risk of kidney disease progression** (FLOW trial, 2024).
- In patients with overweight/obesity and non-diabetic CKD, semaglutide treatment for 24 weeks resulted in a **clinically meaningful reduction in albuminuria** (PubMed, 2024).
- Semaglutide reduced the risk of major kidney outcomes and death from cardiovascular causes in patients with type 2 diabetes and CKD (NEJM, 2024).
- Weight loss achieved with semaglutide is associated with improved estimated glomerular filtration rate (eGFR) outcomes in CKD patients.
- No significant adverse renal events have been reported with semaglutide use in this population.

**Recommendation:** Based on current evidence, semaglutide is recommended as a therapeutic option for obesity management in patients with CKD (Grade A for patients with type 2 diabetes and CKD; Grade B for patients with non-diabetic CKD and obesity).

## Evidence Analysis (with Recommendation Grades)

### 1. Primary Efficacy Outcomes

#### 1.1 Kidney Disease Progression
- **FLOW Trial (2024):** Semaglutide reduced the risk of major kidney outcomes by **24%** in patients with type 2 diabetes and CKD, regardless of disease severity. This landmark trial provides robust evidence for nephroprotection.
- **Recommendation Grade: A** (High-quality evidence from randomized controlled trials)

#### 1.2 Albuminuria Reduction
- **24-Week Randomized Trial (PubMed, 2024):** In patients with overweight/obesity and non-diabetic CKD, semaglutide treatment led to a clinically meaningful reduction in albuminuria, suggesting direct renoprotective effects independent of glycemic control.
- **Recommendation Grade: B** (Moderate-quality evidence; single trial with limited duration)

#### 1.3 Cardiovascular Outcomes
- **Semaglutide Cardiovascular Outcomes Trial (2022):** Reduced major adverse cardiovascular events (MACE) in high-risk patients, including those with CKD.
- **Recommendation Grade: A** (Consistent evidence from multiple RCTs)

### 2. Secondary Outcomes

#### 2.1 Weight Loss and eGFR Improvement
- **Obesity Management in CKD Patients (2024):** Weight loss achieved with semaglutide correlates with improved eGFR outcomes, supporting the mechanistic link between obesity reduction and renal function preservation.
- **Recommendation Grade: B** (Observational and secondary analyses)

#### 2.2 Safety Profile
- **GLP-1 Safety in Renal Impairment (2023):** No significant adverse renal events were reported. Semaglutide is generally well-tolerated, with gastrointestinal side effects being the most common.
- **Recommendation Grade: A** (Consistent safety data across populations)

### 3. Meta-Analysis Evidence
- **GLP-1 RA Meta-analysis (2023):** Pooled analysis of GLP-1 RAs, including semaglutide, demonstrated a **15% reduction in kidney risk** (composite of eGFR decline, end-stage kidney disease, or renal death).
- **Recommendation Grade: A** (High-quality meta-analysis)

## Limitations and Knowledge Gaps

### 1. Study Limitations
- **Duration of follow-up:** Most trials have follow-up periods of 1–3 years; long-term renal outcomes (e.g., progression to end-stage kidney disease) require extended observation.
- **Population heterogeneity:** The majority of evidence comes from patients with type 2 diabetes and CKD. Data in non-diabetic CKD patients with obesity are limited to a single 24-week trial.
- **Dose optimization:** Optimal dosing of semaglutide for renal protection in CKD patients (especially those with eGFR <30 mL/min/1.73 m˛) has not been fully established.

### 2. Knowledge Gaps
- **Mechanism of nephroprotection:** Whether the renal benefits are primarily driven by weight loss, glycemic control, or direct anti-inflammatory/anti-fibrotic effects remains unclear.
- **Combination therapy:** The efficacy and safety of semaglutide in combination with other renoprotective agents (e.g., SGLT2 inhibitors, ACE inhibitors/ARBs) require further investigation.
- **Advanced CKD (Stage 4–5):** Safety and efficacy data in patients with eGFR <30 mL/min/1.73 m˛ are scarce; most trials excluded these patients.
- **Pediatric and adolescent populations:** No data are available on semaglutide use for obesity in CKD in younger patients.
- **Cost-effectiveness:** Formal health economic analyses in CKD-specific populations are lacking.

### 3. Methodological Considerations
- **Publication bias:** Positive results may be overrepresented in the literature; negative or neutral trials may be unpublished.
- **Industry sponsorship:** Many trials are funded by the manufacturer (Novo Nordisk), which may influence study design and reporting.
